Take a Depression Test

Taking a depression test can be a good starting point for identifying and addressing potential mental health issues. Depression is a serious and common mental health disorder that can have a wide-ranging impact on a person's life, including relationships, work, and overall well-being. A depression test can help people understand their symptoms and determine whether they should seek additional professional help. It is important to remember, however, that a depression test is not a replacement for a proper diagnosis by a trained mental health professional. Anyone experiencing depression symptoms should consult with a healthcare provider.

Take a Test

Instructions: Below is a list of questions about life experiences that are common among people who have been diagnosed with depression. Please carefully read each question and indicate how frequently you have faced the same or similar challenges in the last few months.

What is your level of interest or pleasure in doing things currently?

Do you currently feel down, depressed, or hopeless?

Are you having trouble falling or staying asleep, or sleeping too much currently?

Do you currently feel tired or have little energy?

Are you currently experiencing poor appetite or overeating?

Do you currently feel bad about yourself or feel like you have let yourself or your family down?

Do you currently have trouble concentrating on things, such as reading the newspaper or watching television?

Do you currently move or speak slowly to the point where others notice?

Are you currently having thoughts that you would be better off dead or of hurting yourself?

If you are currently experiencing any of these issues, how difficult are they making your work, home, school, or personal relationships?

It's something important you want to know.

Depression is becoming more common nowadays, with an increasing number of people being diagnosed with the condition. High levels of stress, isolation and loneliness, social media pressure, and the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic are all factors. The current state of the world has made it more difficult for people to obtain necessary mental health care, resulting in an increase in untreated depression. Untreated depression can have serious consequences, lowering a person's quality of life and increasing the risk of suicide. As a result, it is critical for individuals to seek assistance and for society to prioritise mental health care.

About Us

"MindEase: Your Depression Management Companion" is a digital tool designed to assist individuals in managing their depression symptoms. The goal of MindEase is to empower people suffering from depression to take control of their mental health and improve their overall well-being.
